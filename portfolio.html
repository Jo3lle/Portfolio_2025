Project 1 Details

For this project, I worked on creating a **manually controlled LED system**. The goal was to design a simple circuit where the LEDs could be turned on and off manually using switches or buttons.

  • ✅ Individual LED control
  • ✅ Manual switching
  • ✅ Clear indication of on/off states
Implementation and Approach

I built the circuit using LEDs, switches, resistors, and a power source. Each LED was connected through its own switch, allowing manual control of its state.

No programming was involved since the control was purely manual. The focus was on wiring the components correctly and ensuring safe operation of the LEDs using appropriate resistors.

Challenges and Problem Solving

Working independently, I was responsible for the entire project:

  • 🔹 **Component selection** (LEDs, switches, resistors)
  • 🔹 **Circuit assembly** and wiring
  • 🔹 **Testing** each LED to ensure correct manual control

The main challenges involved ensuring correct wiring to avoid shorts and verifying that the LEDs lit up properly without overloading the circuit.

Conclusion

This project helped me understand basic electronic circuits and manual control of components. I successfully created a functional LED control system without any programming, relying purely on hardware.

Project 1 Details

For this project, I worked on the development of an **Arduino-controlled car**. The goal was to program a fully functional control system that could move the car in **all directions**:

  • ✅ Forward
  • ✅ Backward
  • ✅ Left
  • ✅ Right
Implementation and Approach

I started completely from scratch, which meant I had to write and implement all the code myself. The control was made possible using servos that directed the car’s wheels.

I programmed everything in the Arduino IDE, using the Servo.h library for precise control. Through trial and error, I improved the car’s movements and optimized the code for faster and smoother steering.

Challenges and Problem Solving

Working independently, I was responsible for every part of the project:

  • 🔹 **Hardware setup** (servos and wiring)
  • 🔹 **Writing code** and testing movements
  • 🔹 **Analyzing and optimizing** performance

Throughout the project, I faced challenges such as **inaccurate servo control** and **delayed responses**, but I managed to solve these through **debugging and optimization**.

Conclusion

This project gave me valuable insights into **servo control, Arduino programming, and problem solving**. In the end, I successfully created a working, controllable car with **fully self-written code**.

Project 3 Details

This project was an individual soldering exercise, where I soldered 13 LEDs and 13 transistors to create a blinking light effect.

🔧 Components Used
  • 💡 13 LEDs
  • ⚡ 13 transistors
  • 🔩 Resistors and wiring
  • 🔧 Soldering iron and solder
  • 🔋 Power supply (9V or 12V)
🛠 Execution and Method

The soldering process consisted of the following steps:

  • ✅ Correctly connecting the LEDs and transistors to the circuit board.
  • ✅ Using resistors to prevent overcurrent.
  • ✅ Soldering the components precisely to create a solid circuit.
  • ✅ Testing if the LEDs blink correctly by supplying power.
📌 Lessons Learned

Through this project, I developed the following skills:

  • 🔹 Precision and neatness in soldering.
  • 🔹 Correctly placing LEDs and transistors in a circuit.
  • 🔹 Understanding how a blinking light circuit works.
🎯 Conclusion

This soldering project not only helped me improve my soldering techniques but also gave me insight into the use of transistors and resistors within an electronic circuit. The final result was a blinking LED pattern, providing a nice visual representation of how electricity and circuits work together.

Summer breeze

For this project, I created a digital artwork called "Ashtrap Art" using Adobe Illustrator. The piece was designed with a summer theme in mind, combining colors and elements that reflect warmth, sunlight, and relaxation.

🎨 Concept and Color Choices

The main color I used was dark blue, symbolizing the deep summer sky or ocean. To contrast that, I added bright orange tones, representing the warmth of the sun and the energy of summer days. These contrasting colors gave the artwork both depth and vibrancy.

I also included a glass element in the design—like a glass of water or a summer drink—bringing a refreshing, cooling touch to the otherwise hot-toned image. This created a visual balance and helped communicate the idea of refreshment during summer heat.

🖥 Tools and Techniques
  • 🧩 Created using Adobe Illustrator
  • 🎨 Used gradients and layer effects for lighting
  • 🌞 Focused on warm vs cool color contrast
  • 💡 Inspiration from real summer scenery and abstract art
📌 Reflection

This project allowed me to experiment with color psychology and digital composition. It was interesting to see how combining cool and warm tones could evoke strong seasonal feelings. "Ashtrap Art" became a creative expression of how summer feels—hot, bright, and refreshing all at once.

Coraline filmposter

For this project, I had to design a movie poster based on an existing film. I chose *Coraline*, a film known for its eerie atmosphere and unique visual style. The task was to create an **original poster**, not a copy of the existing one. This project was assigned at my previous school.

🎨 Concept and Creative Choices

I decided to reimagine the film with a completely different visual style. I drew Coraline myself, using **new colors and tone** to give it a fresh, yet still creepy, appearance.

  • 🏚️ I added a **creepy house** in the background to set the mood.
  • 🌳 Next to it, I drew a **twisted, scary tree** to build suspense.
  • 🌕 Behind everything, the **moon** shines bright—but if you look closely, you’ll see **four buttons** inside it.

These four buttons are a symbolic element, representing the main themes of the movie—control, illusion, danger, and choice. Using Adobe Illustrator, I carefully composed the layout and colors to reflect both mystery and beauty.

💡 What I Learned
  • 🖌️ How to visually reinterpret an existing story.
  • 🎨 Using **color theory** to create mood and tension.
  • 🧠 Thinking creatively under project guidelines.

This project helped me grow as a designer and storyteller, while giving me the freedom to explore **creepy aesthetics** and **symbolism** through illustration.

Website Design for Wijkcentrum de Burgerij

For this project, I was responsible for designing a full website for a real community center called De Burgerij. The goal was to create a clean and user-friendly website layout that reflected the atmosphere and purpose of the community center.

🎯 Project Goal

The objective was to visualize the layout, pages, and user experience before development. I worked closely with their team to make sure the design matched their vision and needs.

🖥 Pages and Structure
  • 📍 Homepage with introduction and news
  • 📅 Activity calendar
  • 📂 Information pages per department
  • 🗺️ Location and contact page with an interactive map
📐 Tools and Process

I used tools like Adobe XD and Figma to build wireframes and visual mockups. After several design reviews, the website was developed and is now live and publicly accessible.

🌐 Result

The final design was published online and is now actively being used by visitors of De Burgerij. You can even find it online by searching for their official site!
